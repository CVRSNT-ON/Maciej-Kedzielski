The Artist

Self-taught.
Instinct-driven.

Maciej Kędzielski was born in 1983 in Katowice, Poland's industrial heartland. He came to painting not through formal instruction but through the necessity of making sense of experience — his most reliable guide has always been intuition.

His early work was graphic and monochrome: abstract forms evoking the architecture of the human head, rendered in pencil and ink. Around 2024 he moved decisively into colour. What followed was an expansion of both palette and ambition.

Working in Kraków, Kędzielski builds his canvases through accumulated gestures — each layer a record of decision and doubt, pressure and release. The resulting works sit firmly within expressionism, but they resist easy categorisation. They are too physical for the decorative, too intuitive for the conceptual.

Process

Intuition as the only reliable
compass

The Mark

Every painting begins without a plan. Kędzielski starts from a physical impulse — a direction, a colour, a pressure on the brush. Each mark invites the next and refuses the one that doesn't belong.

The Colour

Since his shift to paint in 2024, colour has become the primary language. Terracottas and greens, sky blues and raw umbers build compositions that feel instinctively balanced despite their apparent chaos.

The Surface

Layers accumulate and are partially destroyed. Passages are covered, scraped, re-marked. The final surface carries the history of the painting's making — nothing is hidden.

The Form

Forms emerge from the process rather than preceding it. Earlier, these were heads. Now they are less specific — energy, weight, direction. The human presence is felt rather than depicted.
